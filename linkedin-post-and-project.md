## Current LinkedIn Notes

According to LinkedIn Help:

- `Projects` is currently available under `Add profile section > Recommended > Projects`.
- For newly added projects, LinkedIn notes that the `Project URL` field is not available, and recommends using `Add media > Add a link` instead.
- The `Featured` section can include your own posts, external links, media, and documents.

Helpful LinkedIn sources:

- Projects and profile sections: `https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a540837/add-sections-to-your-profile`
- Featured section: `https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a550399/manage-featured-samples-of-your-work-on-your-linkedin-profile?lang=en`
- Featured FAQs: `https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a552452/featured-section-on-your-profile-faqs`
- Document posts: `https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a518909`

## Recommended Order

1. Publish the post.
2. Add the portfolio to `Projects`.
3. Add the post and/or portfolio link to `Featured`.

## Better Post Option

If you want, the next strong move is a LinkedIn carousel post:

- Slide 1: title and your name
- Slide 2: portfolio overview
- Slide 3: software projects
- Slide 4: systems support and internship experience
- Slide 5: leadership and community impact
- Slide 6: QR code or portfolio link

That usually performs better than a plain link post.
